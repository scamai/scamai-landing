Following methodology developed by [NIST](https://www.nist.gov) (National Institute of Standards and Technology) and adapted for UK consumers by CheckReal AI, this framework provides actionable steps for identifying potentially manipulated media.

### Step 1: Contextual Verification

Before analyzing the media itself, verify the source and context:

1. Check the original upload location and timestamp
2. Verify the account's posting history and verification status
3. Cross-reference the claim across multiple reputable UK news sources (BBC, Guardian, Independent)
4. Use [Full Fact](https://fullfact.org) or [FactCheck.org](https://factcheck.org) for viral claims

### Step 2: Visual Analysis

Examine the media for these specific indicators:

- **Facial inconsistencies**: Unnatural eye movements, missing blink patterns, or asymmetric lighting
- **Audio quality**: Pay attention to voice modulation, background noise, or unnatural cadence
- **Movement anomalies**: Flickering around the face edges, body proportions, or shadow inconsistencies
- **Texture detection**: Look for blurring or artifacts around hairline and ears

### Step 3: Technical Verification

### Step 4: Source Documentation

Document your findings systematically:

### Step 5: Reporting Pathway

If deepfake content is identified:

1. Report to the platform hosting the content
2. Submit to [Action Fraud UK](https://actionfraud.police.uk) if financial fraud is involved
